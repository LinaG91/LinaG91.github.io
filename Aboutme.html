About Me

Welcome to my website! I'm Hlina Getachew, originally from Addis Ababa, Ethiopia. My passion for technology began in high school, but it wasn't until recently that I discovered my true calling. Growing up in Addis, I was always captivated by how things work, sparking my interest in technology at a young age. I earned a teaching degree and worked as a teacher for six months before making the life-changing decision to move to the United States.

After arriving in the U.S., I worked as a housekeeper at Nazareth College. Despite the challenges of adjusting to a new language and culture, I was determined to continue my education. At Monroe Community College (MCC), I pursued Information Technology while juggling two jobs—working weekends at Starbucks and serving as a student assistant on campus.

My time at MCC was transformative. I earned a spot on the Dean’s List for over four semesters, joined the Phi Theta Kappa Honor Society, and received consecutive congratulatory letters from Monroe County. After completing my associate’s degree, I transferred to SUNY Brockport to pursue a bachelor’s degree in Cybersecurity, which excites me because it combines my love for technology with the crucial task of protecting information in today’s digital world.

This website is a reflection of my journey, education, and aspirations. Throughout my career, I’ve gained hands-on experience with programming languages like Java and HTML, building a strong foundation for my future in technology.

Below, you’ll find a YouTube video of one of the proudest moments of my journey—being interviewed by the MCC Foundations as a successful student. It was an incredible honor to be chosen, and this recognition is a testament to my hard work and dedication to both my studies and personal growth.
